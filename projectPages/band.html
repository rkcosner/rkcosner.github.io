MATLab Algorithms for Planning the Marching Band's Formations



      In the Spring of 2016, UC Berkeley's E7 Introduction to Computer Programming for Engineers, the class was tasked with developing an algorithm to aid the marching band in planning their on-field performances.

      In particular, the band needed a way to traverse from one formation to another within a certain number of beats without any collisions between bandsmen. The class then had to develop a program which would take the beginning and ending formations as inputs and plan a route for each bandsman.

      This problem was particularly interesting to me because, as a member of the marching band, I knew how useful this kind of program could be, reducing the time which is required to plan shows which had previously taken a team of 6 several days for every football game.

      Eventually, the rest of the class and I were able to develop a variety fo solutions which were then given to the band and are currently in use to help improve our process in designing shows.
