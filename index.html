Projects

June 2017 - Present: This Website

A website built, from scratch without a traditional Wordpress/Weebly/ETC template. Designed and coded entirely from the ground up: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and all.


June 2017 - Present : Electric Skateboard

A high torque motorized skateboard which features a Wii-Nunchuck remote, a fully programmable arduino controller, and 3D printed housings.


Summer 2017: Teaching Abroad in Peru

Lessons teaching basic Computer Science (coding), Electrical Engineering (Arduino based), and Mechanical Engineering Design (Fusion360 and 3D printing) for Colegio Santa Maria in Chincha, Peru.

Summer 2017: Computer Mouse for the Handicapped

This was one of the most significant projects that I worked on while in Peru. It is a computer mouse which quadrepalegic people can control with their mouth and breath. Although it was not an original idea, I learned a lot from having to work with only the limited tools that we had with us.

June 2017 - Present : 250mm FPV Quadcopter

A high-speed, lightweight quadcopter with First-Person-View (FPV) built from the groundup with the intent of eventually adding altitude-hold capabilities to film and aid the UC, Berkeley Marching Band or the Mira Costa High School Marching Band.


Spring 2017: Fluid Flow

Various fluid studies including a Lagrangian particle flow model developed in MATLAB, a fluid flow study done using Ansys Fluent, and a flow experiment using dry-ice and a variety of fluid visualization techniques.

Spring 2017: 3D Printed Bridge

A lightweight, highly-toleranced, and rigid 12 inch bridge manufactured in 5 parts using a 3D printer and PLA material. Capable of supporting ~350lbs using only the frictional force created by two veritcal mounting poles.

Summer 2016 and Spring 2017: Wave Energy Research

Research on load propagation due to ocean waves rolling over rigid platforms, particularly in stormy and extreme weather conditions. Also, component testing to determine moments of inertia, spring constants, max-torques, etc. as well as mooring design.

Summer 2016: 3D Printed Housing for Microfluidics Device

A 3D printed housing device used to hold various components of a microfluidic cancer cell detection system.

Spring 2016: MATLAB Algorithms for the Marching Band

An algorithm developed to help the University of California Marching Band plan their movements and avoid colisions during their during their halftime shows.

Spring 2016: 3D Printed Wind Turbine

A NACA Airfowl-based, high-efficiency, 3D-printed wind turbine and support structure.

Fall 2015 - Present: Human Powered Vehicles

"Super Bikes" meant to go as fast as possible without any motor or engine. These vehicles are built by a team at Berkeley which competes annually in the ASME competition and is shooting to break the unassisted land speed record at Battle Mountain.

Summer 2015: Novel Genetic Disease Testing

Research regarding MPSI (Mucopolysacharidosis Type 1) and our ability to detect efective treatment based on measurements of lysosomal proteins; a method much easier and safer than the traditional method of using radioactive iodine.

Summer 2014: Carbon Nanotube Research

Conducted research on the thermal properties of carbon nanotubes to determine possible uses for them on satellites.

Summer 2014: Quick Prototyping Tool

A tactile tool to be used in the early development and planning stages of satellite dsign in Boeing's Satellite Design Center.

Spring 2014: HVS Mariba

Hermosa Valley School's Marimba and My Eagle Project. A fully functional musical instrument and also my first experiments with CAD.


November 2013 - Present: 1980 Mazda RX7

A 1980 Mazda RX7 with a Wankel Rotary Engine and replaced or refurbished electrical system, radio, antenna, exhaust, brakes, steering rack, windows, doors, locks, suspension, and transmission, amongst other things.


Spring 2013: Science Olympiad Trebuchet

The first project that I completed in which I really applied science that I learned in school, recorded data, and performed some stastistical analysis: A accurate projectile device designed for the Southern California Science Olympiad Competition.
